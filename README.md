<section id="chapter1">
<h2>Chapter 1 · How to Think Like a Bakery R&D Technologist</h2>
<p class="lede">Bakery R&D is not just making a cake better — it is learning why the cake changed.</p>

<div class="grid2">
  <div class="card"><span class="chip gold">Baking Mindset</span><h3>Repeat the recipe</h3><p>A baker follows a method and judges whether the final cake is good.</p></div>
  <div class="card"><span class="chip green">R&D Mindset</span><h3>Explain the result</h3><p>An R&D technologist controls variables, records evidence, compares samples and explains cause and effect.</p></div>
</div>

<h2>Professional Thinking Flow</h2>
<table>
<tr><th>Step</th><th>Question</th><th>Output</th></tr>
<tr><td>Observation</td><td>What do I see?</td><td>Crumb, height, colour, texture, mouthfeel.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Evidence</td><td>What data supports it?</td><td>Formula, temperature, mixing time, photos.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Hypothesis</td><td>What may have caused it?</td><td>Ingredient, process or baking condition.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Experiment</td><td>How do I test it?</td><td>Change one variable only.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Conclusion</td><td>What did I learn?</td><td>Document result and next test.</td></tr>
</table>

<div class="note"><b>R&D Rule</b>If you change flour, sugar, mixing time and oven temperature at the same time, the result may improve, but you will not know why.</div>
</section>

<h2>Troubleshooting Atlas · Tunneling in Cakes</h2>
<p class="lede">When you see long tube-like holes inside a cake crumb, the defect is called tunneling.</p>

<div class="grid2">
  <div><span class="chip green">Good crumb</span><div class="photo"></div></div>
  <div><span class="chip red">Tunneled crumb</span><div class="photo"></div></div>
</div>

<table>
<tr><th>What you see</th><th>Likely cause</th><th>Fix</th></tr>
<tr><td>Long vertical holes + chewy bite</td><td>Overmixing after flour addition</td><td>Reduce final mixing time.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Large holes + soapy taste</td><td>Too much chemical leavener</td><td>Reduce baking powder or soda.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Dense elastic crumb</td><td>Flour protein too strong</td><td>Use lower-protein flour or adjust formula.</td></tr>
</table>
</section>

<section id="dictionary">
<h2>Technical Dictionary</h2>
<div class="grid2">
  <div class="card"><span class="chip red">Cake Defect</span><h3>Tunneling</h3><p><b>Plain English:</b> Long tube-like holes inside cake crumb.<br><b>Professional:</b> Vertical channels formed when over-developed gluten traps escaping gas.</p></div>
  <div class="card"><span class="chip amber">Starch Term</span><h3>Gelatinisation</h3><p><b>Plain English:</b> Starch swells and thickens when heated with water.<br><b>Importance:</b> Helps cake structure set.</p></div>
  <div class="card"><span class="chip blue">Thermal Term</span><h3>Conduction</h3><p><b>Plain English:</b> Heat moving through direct contact.<br><b>Example:</b> Hot pan transferring heat into cake batter.</p></div>
  <div class="card"><span class="chip gold">R&D Term</span><h3>Control Sample</h3><p><b>Plain English:</b> The unchanged sample used for comparison.<br><b>Purpose:</b> Shows whether your test variable caused the result.</p></div>
</div>
</section>
